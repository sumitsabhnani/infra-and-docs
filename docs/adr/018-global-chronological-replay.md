# ADR-018: Global Chronological Replay — Connected-Component Valuation Engine

**Status:** Accepted — `app.features.global-replay` flag and legacy per-master AVCO paths removed in fc26b7b/43dc768; Holdings now consume the engine; spin-off ingest replumbed (see ADR-024).
**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Context:** Phase B of the corporate-actions roadmap closes two gaps the per-master AVCO walker could not model: **mergers** (cost basis must move from security A to security B at an exact UTC ex_date, optionally with a cash-boot realization) and **spin-offs** (a fraction of parent basis must move to a newly-appearing child master while parent shares stay unchanged). Both are structurally cross-master state transitions — the pre-Phase-B engine processed transactions per-`security_master_id` in isolation, so every consumer (`HistoryQueryService`, `StockGroupService`, `TickerDetailService`) looped per master and never saw two masters in one walk. A user who held AT&T through the 2022 WBD spin-off saw arithmetically wrong realized P/L on the WBD leg: no basis had been transferred. Fixing this required rewriting the AVCO loop, not bolting a handler onto it. This ADR records the architectural shift and its two non-obvious design decisions: (a) connected-component replay as the new walk shape; (b) ephemeral ledger entries as the audit surface, instead of extending the immutable ledger enum.

---

## Decision 1: Replay Unit Is a Connected Component, Not a `security_master_id`

**Choice:** The valuation engine now groups `security_master_id`s into **replay chains** — connected components of the graph `(V = masters in scope, E = merger edges from→to ∪ spin-off edges parent→child)`. A new `CorporateActionChainResolver` computes chains via bidirectional BFS over `corporate_action_merger` and `corporate_action_spinoff` starting from the portfolio's transaction-derived seed master ids. `PortfolioReplayService.replay(portfolioId, reportingCurrency, fxSnapshot)` walks each chain as a single merged timeline `(transactions ∪ splits ∪ mergers ∪ spin-offs)` ordered by `(effective UTC timestamp, eventKindOrdinal)` — splits before mergers before spin-offs before transactions at the same instant. Per-master `AvcoState`s mutate in-place; cross-master events call the three new `AvcoState` mutators added in this phase: `mergeFrom(other, ratioFactor)`, `applyCashBoot(cashPerShare, cashBasisPct)`, `splitOffFraction(basisPct, childSharesPerParent)`.

**Crucially, trivial chains — a master with no merger/spin-off edges — delegate directly to the legacy `RealizedGainCalculator.computeEpisodesAndSellRealized / computeReportingCurrency / computeTxnCurrency / detectAnomalies` entry points.** This guarantees bit-for-bit parity on merger-free portfolios; the new code path runs only when a merger or spin-off actually exists in scope. `ReplayParityTest` asserts byte-identical `BigDecimal` realized P/L, episode field-equality, anomaly field-equality, and empty ephemeral-entry sets across a seeded 100+-transaction, 5-security portfolio that exercises splits and stock dividends but no mergers. The parity is by construction, not by coincidence: trivial chains run the exact legacy methods, verbatim.

**Rationale:** The alternative considered was **repurposing `canonicalMasterId`** as a time-scoped alias for mergers. Rejected because `canonicalMasterId` is a bidirectional, eternal same-security alias (ADR-005) used by 7+ consumers including `TransactionRepository.findAllForReplay` (`t.security.id = :id OR t.security.canonicalMasterId = :id`), every `SecurityMaster.getEffectiveId()` call in the AVCO path, and 3+ native SQL queries that `COALESCE(canonical_master_id, id)`. Reusing it for mergers would make pre-merger A transactions bleed into pre-merger B analytics — a silent correctness regression with no test that could catch it because the bidirectional aliasing semantics pre-date Phase B. The time-scoped chain table stays separate; `canonicalMasterId` stays a static same-security deduplication key and is untouched.

**The chain is the scope of replay, not the scope of any stored relationship.** `ReplayChain` exists only in memory during a request; the on-disk representation is two independent tables (`corporate_action_merger`, `corporate_action_spinoff`), each a one-to-one declaration of a single edge. Cycles (shouldn't exist in real data) are detected by the BFS and collapsed into a single component without infinite walk. The `app.features.global-replay` flag gates the new path: `false` routes callers through the legacy per-master loop; `true` routes through `PortfolioReplayService.replay`. Both paths are retained for the duration of shadow-mode validation; `SymbolChangeNoOpIntegrationTest` asserts that an ADR-005 `canonicalMasterId` alias emits zero ephemeral rows under the new path, proving the two invariants stay disjoint.

## Decision 2: Ephemeral `CorporateActionLedgerEntry` — Audit Without Ledger Mutation

**Choice:** Cross-master events emit `CorporateActionLedgerEntry` records — in-memory, never persisted — that surface on the History page as synthetic rows. Five entry kinds:

- `MERGER_TRANSFER_OUT` on master A: shares and basis leave.
- `MERGER_TRANSFER_IN` on master B: shares and basis arrive at the declared ratio.
- `MERGER_CASH_BOOT` on master A: realized P/L on the cash portion (IRC §356 — cash is proceeds against allocated basis).
- `SPINOFF_BASIS_OUT` on parent P: basis leaves, quantity unchanged.
- `SPINOFF_CREATION` on child C: `parentQty × sharesPerParent` shares arrive with the moved basis.

Paired entries from the same source event share a stable `sourceEventId` (the `corporate_action_merger.id` or `corporate_action_spinoff.id`). `HistoryQueryService` maps them to `TransactionHistoryRow` with a new `synthetic: Boolean` field, `transactionType = entry.kind().name()`, and `transactionDate = ex_date T00:00:00Z` — identical to the splits ordering convention in ADR-014, so any real transaction on or after the ex_date is post-event in the merged timeline. The frontend renders synthetic rows with a distinct chip color (purple for merger-family, pink for spin-off-family), a lock icon, and a basis-transfer tooltip; the rows are keyed by `sourceEventId` so DOM identity survives re-renders.

**Rationale:** The obvious alternative — add `MERGER_TRANSFER_OUT`/etc. to `Transaction.TransactionType` and insert synthetic transaction rows — was rejected because it would breach the Immutable Ledger principle (SYSTEM_SNAPSHOT §4). An `enum` value on the persisted ledger is one migration away from a mock row being written to `transactions` by accident; the failure mode is a silent double-count the Accounting Tie-Out Principle can't detect (the ledger would sum to itself correctly). Keeping the persisted enum pristine and using a parallel DTO type means the ledger remains what it has always been: exactly the rows the broker reported, plus nothing. The precedent is already established by `MissingTransactionRow` (ADR-013): ephemeral audit surface, no backing `transactions.id`, emitted by the walker.

**The Accounting Tie-Out Principle is preserved, not abandoned, by this choice.** Dashboard realized P/L (`StockGroupService.computeTotalRealizedGain` under the flag) routes through `PortfolioReplayService.replay(...).totalRealizedReporting()`, which sums per-master realized — including cash-boot realized attributed to the surrendered master A per IRC §356. History page per-SELL realized comes from the same `perSellRealizedReporting` map on the same `ReplayResult`. A user looking at `Dashboard → Realized` and `History → (sum of SELL realized) + (sum of MERGER_CASH_BOOT realized)` sees the same number because both surfaces read from the same chain walk. `StockGroupRealizedMergerIntegrationTest` asserts exactly this: `totalRealizedReporting == realizedA + realizedB` across a cash-boot merger chain.

## Decision 3: Spin-off Basis Allocation Is Manual-Primary, EODHD Is Metadata-Only

**Choice:** `corporate_action_spinoff.basis_allocation_pct` is **nullable**. EODHD fundamentals provide `(ex_date, shares_per_parent, child_provider_symbol)` but never the IRS Form 8937 `basis_allocation_pct`. The `EodhdSpinoffClient` parses what it can and the `CorporateActionSpinoffBackfillJob` persists rows with `basis_allocation_pct = NULL` and `source = "EODHD_PARTIAL"`. The replay walker treats a null pct as no-op (skip the basis transfer) and is designed to emit a future `SPINOFF_MISSING_BASIS` anomaly so the admin supplies the pct via `POST /api/admin/corporate-actions/spinoffs/manual` — the manual endpoint upgrades an EODHD_PARTIAL row in place (idempotent on `(parent, child, ex_date)`) by setting the pct and flipping the source to `MANUAL`. Mergers, by contrast, auto-derive cash-basis allocation from `cash_per_share / (cash_per_share + ratio × to_master_ex_date_close_price)` at replay time when available (future PR wiring `MarketDataPriceDailyRepository`), with a nullable `cash_basis_pct_override` admin escape hatch for Form-8937-specific allocations.

**Rationale:** Form 8937 filings are authoritative for tax accounting; EODHD's spin-off data is demonstrably unreliable on the pct field (empirically never populated). Letting ingest write a null is correct — it signals "event detected but not actionable" without ever fabricating a wrong number. The primary user flow is therefore: EODHD backfill seeds the event metadata (ex_date, ratio, counterparties), the admin audits against Form 8937 and supplies the pct. This inverts the merger ingest story (where EODHD is mostly-usable) and matches the declared-vs-actual discipline established in ADR-017 §Decision 1.

## Consequences

- **Zero perf cost on typical portfolios.** A 50-position portfolio with no mergers collapses to 50 trivial 1-node chains; each delegates to the byte-identical legacy walk. Only portfolios containing merger/spin-off affected masters pay the cross-master walk cost.
- **Feature flag is the deploy surface, not a permanent switch.** `app.features.global-replay=false` is the default until the parity envelope is observed in staging, then flipped on. Removing the legacy per-master loop is a follow-up PR after the flag is burned in; retaining both paths right now is a deliberate safety property, not technical debt.
- **`transactions` remains strictly append-only.** No migration adds synthetic rows, no schema change touches the ledger enum, no back-write occurs for any corporate action. Mergers, spin-offs, splits, and cash dividends all follow the same discipline: declared events live in their own tables, and they affect user-visible numbers exclusively through read-time computation (the AVCO walk for basis-mutating events; `DividendAnalyticsService` for cash-visible events).
- **Per-master realized attribution is opinionated by IRS rules.** Cash-boot realized is attributed to the surrendered master A (IRC §356) — US-centric. Other jurisdictions may disagree; documented as a Phase B scope assumption. Downstream tax-export features can re-attribute if needed.
- **Ephemeral rows are stable across requests.** `sourceEventId` keys DOM identity; paired `OUT`/`IN` rows at the same ex_date ordinal carry the same id. Refreshing the History page doesn't reshuffle the frontend's row tracking.
- **Chain resolver is the shared discovery mechanism.** Every surface that needs to know "what masters must walk together" routes through `CorporateActionChainResolver.resolveChains`. Future extensions (rights offerings, stock-for-stock buyouts that aren't already modeled as mergers) add new edge types to the same graph; the walk shape and the ephemeral-entry mechanism stay unchanged.
- **Symbol changes stay outside Phase B.** `canonicalMasterId` aliasing, the ADR-005 deduplication mechanism, continues to collapse cross-exchange and ticker-rename listings into one canonical master before any replay begins. `SymbolChangeNoOpIntegrationTest` proves a canonical alias added mid-portfolio emits zero ephemeral rows and preserves the legacy realized number exactly — symbol changes and corporate actions are two orthogonal mechanisms sharing the same aggregation key (`SecurityMaster.getEffectiveId()`) without overlapping semantics.
